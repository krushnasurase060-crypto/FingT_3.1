# 🎹 Piano Keyboard Mapping - Simplified Guide

## 📍 NEW SIMPLIFIED LAYOUT

The piano keyboard mapping has been completely redesigned for **simplicity and ease of learning**.

### How It Works

Your keyboard is divided into **2 octaves**:

---

## **LOWER OCTAVE (C4 to B4)**

### White Keys (Natural Notes)
Press these bottom row keys:

| Z | X | C | V | B | N | M |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | D | E | F | G | A | B |

**Example:** Press `Z` to play middle C

### Black Keys (Sharp/Flat Notes)  
Press these number keys:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| C# | D# | F# | G# | A# |

---

## **HIGHER OCTAVE (C5 to B5)**

### White Keys (Natural Notes)
Press these top row keys:

| Q | W | E | R | T | Y | U |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | D | E | F | G | A | B |

### Black Keys (Sharp/Flat Notes)
Press these number keys:

| 6 | 7 | 8 | 9 | 0 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| C# | D# | F# | G# | A# |

---

## ✨ Why This Layout?

✅ **Sequential** - Keys follow musical order left-to-right  
✅ **Natural** - Mirrors keyboard layout  
✅ **Easy to Remember** - Two rows = two octaves  
✅ **Intuitive** - Numbers 1-5 for lower sharps, 6-0 for upper sharps  
✅ **Beginner Friendly** - No confusing scattered keys  

---

## 🎵 Quick Practice

1. Press `Z` → hear C4
2. Press `1` → hear C#4  
3. Press `X` → hear D4
4. Press `Q` → hear C5 (higher)
5. Press `6` → hear C#5 (higher sharp)

---

## 🔧 Customizing Keys

Want different keyboard shortcuts?

1. Go to **Settings** → **Piano Keyboard Mapping**
2. Click any note button (e.g., "C4")
3. Press any keyboard key you want
4. It's saved automatically!

**Reset:** Click "Reset Piano to Defaults" to go back.

---

## 💡 Tips

- Use **Z-M** for slow, careful practice (lower octave natural)
- Use **Q-U** for faster melodies (higher octave natural)
- Use number keys for sharps when you're ready
- Try playing left-handed: A-Z for lower, S-X-C for sharps!
